Introduction

This e-leaflet provides clear and concise information about over-the-counter cough and cold medications. It covers common medications, safe usage, side effects, drug interactions, and proper storage and disposal. Tailored for adults and seniors, this guide helps you make informed decisions for managing cold symptoms.

Medication Information

Understanding Over-the-Counter (OTC) Cold and Cough Medications

This guide provides information on common OTC medications used to relieve cold and cough symptoms. Always consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice.

List of Common OTC Cough and Cold Medications

  • Decongestants: e.g., Pseudoephedrine and Phenylephrine.
  • Antihistamines: e.g., Diphenhydramine (first-generation) and Loratadine (second-generation).
  • Expectorants: e.g., Guaifenesin helps thin mucus.
  • Cough Suppressants: e.g., Dextromethorphan reduces coughing.
  • Pain Relievers: e.g., Acetaminophen and Ibuprofen relieve pain and fever.
  • Combination Medications: Combine multiple ingredients to tackle various symptoms.

Safe Use and Dosage

  • Read the Label: Follow directions and warnings closely.
  • Dosage: Do not exceed the recommended dose.
  • Age Appropriateness: Check age restrictions; consult a doctor for children under 6 months.
  • Duration: Avoid using nasal sprays beyond 3-5 days to prevent rebound congestion.

Potential Side Effects

OTC medications may cause side effects. Examples include:

  • Decongestants: Increased heart rate, elevated blood pressure, restlessness.
  • Antihistamines: Drowsiness, dry mouth, blurred vision.
  • Expectorants: Nausea and stomach upset.
  • Cough Suppressants: Dizziness, drowsiness, nausea.
  • Pain Relievers: Stomach upset or, with excessive doses, liver damage (acetaminophen).

Drug Interactions Learn more

  • Inform your healthcare provider about all medications you are taking.
  • Check labels for warnings about interactions.
  • Avoid taking multiple products with the same active ingredients.

Storage and Disposal Learn more

  • Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
  • Keep out of reach of children and pets.
  • Dispose of medications via take-back programs or as per label instructions.
